Game Modes

Three ways to play. All built for groups.

Every mode is designed for private pools of 5 to 100 people. Single-tournament sprints or season-long campaigns — your call.

⛳

Tiered Pick

Players are ranked into tiers using DataGolf Rankings in 10-rank increments. Pick one player per tier. The core Albatross experience.

  • Tiers based on real DataGolf rankings (T1 = ranks 1-10, T2 = 11-20, etc.)
  • One pick per tier — build a balanced roster across the field
  • Single-tournament format, 4-day lifecycle
  • Commissioner controls number of tiers and scoring rules
  • Picks lock at tournament start — no mid-round changes
💰

Salary Cap

Build the best roster under a fixed budget. Player salaries are generated from DataGolf projections and live odds data.

  • Salary values derived from real projections and betting odds
  • Fixed budget — spend it wisely across your roster
  • Single-tournament format with flexible roster sizes
  • Commissioner sets budget amount and roster constraints
  • Strategy: balance stars and sleepers within your cap
🏆

One and Done

Season-long endurance mode. Pick one player per tournament — once you use them, they're locked for the rest of the season.

  • Scoring = real PGA Tour prize money earned (USD)
  • Two variants: Cumulative (total earnings) or Survivor (elimination)
  • Survivor rules: missed cut, worst score, or bottom N — commissioner decides
  • Configurable tournament selection (Majors Only, FedEx Cup, Full Season, Custom)
  • Season archive and renewal for multi-year leagues

FAQ

Questions? We've got answers.

Is Albatross a gambling app?

No. Albatross is a social fantasy golf app for private friend groups. No real money is wagered through the app. The commissioner pays a flat fee to create a pool, and members join for free. Any prize arrangements between friends happen privately outside the app — Albatross doesn't process, facilitate, or take a cut of any winnings.

Is this the same as DraftKings or FanDuel?

No. Albatross is not a daily fantasy sports (DFS) platform. There are no mass-market contests, no entry fees with variable payouts, no house edge, and no pari-mutuel mechanics. It's designed for groups of people who know each other — friends, coworkers, golf buddies.

Why do you show betting odds?

Odds from 40+ bookmakers are displayed in the Research tab as an analytical tool. They help you evaluate player value and compare market sentiment to statistical models. You cannot place bets through Albatross. Odds data is purely for research and informational purposes.

How many people can be in a pool?

Pools support anywhere from 5 to 100 members. Most pools run with 8-20 people — a golf trip group, an office league, or a friend group chat. The commissioner controls who joins via a private invite code.

Who pays? Do all members pay?

Only the commissioner (pool creator) pays — either $4.99 per pool or a monthly/annual subscription for unlimited pools. Every member joins completely free. No ads, no upsells, no "premium tiers" that gate basic features for members.

Where does the data come from?

Player statistics and predictive models come from DataGolf, one of the most respected analytics providers in golf. Live leaderboards and scorecards come from RapidAPI Live Golf Data. Betting odds come from The Odds API, aggregating 40+ bookmakers.

What tournaments can I create pools for?

Any PGA Tour event on the 2026 schedule. For One and Done season-long mode, you can select from presets like Majors Only, FedEx Cup events, Full PGA Tour Season, or hand-pick specific tournaments with the Custom option.

Is there an Android version?

Albatross is launching on iOS first. The app is built with React Native, so an Android version is technically possible and something we're considering for the future based on demand.
